**The Big Picture:** The Department of Defense (DoD) faces a hard **FY2027 deadline** to achieve "Target Level" Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) across its entire enterprise—a mandate encompassing 152 activities across 7 security pillars.

---

### Why It Matters
This is the most complex security migration in history, spanning millions of users and thousands of legacy/cloud networks. Success dictates whether the DoD can secure distributed networks in contested environments or if legacy IT continues to be an open vulnerability.

### The Details: Seven Pillars of DoD Zero Trust
The framework classifies security controls across 7 distinct fields:
- **User:** Enforces phishing-resistant MFA (CAC/PIV) and continuous behavioral monitoring.
- **Device:** Mandates real-time endpoint threat detection and device health attestation.
- **Applications & Workloads:** Scopes access to micro-perimeters and requires Software Bills of Materials (SBOM).
- **Data:** Tagging, labeling, and data loss prevention (DLP) to track Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI).
- **Network:** Enforces network microsegmentation and encrypted transit (SASE/ZTNA).
- **Automation & Orchestration:** Integrates security playbooks (SOAR) for automated response.
- **Visibility & Analytics:** Consolidates logging to identify anomalous behavior.

### Go Deeper: Target vs. Advanced Maturity
DoD components are graded against two distinct execution levels:
1. **Target Level ZT (91 Activities):** The mandatory FY2027 baseline. Focuses on identity, endpoint security, and initial segmentations.
2. **Advanced Level ZT (+61 Activities):** The long-term objective. Assumes fully automated response playbooks, continuous risk analytics, and policy-as-code admission controls.
3. **Implementation Bottlenecks:** While cloud migrations are tracking ahead of schedule, legacy mainframe integrations and physical network microsegmentation remain high risks to meeting the 2027 timeline.
